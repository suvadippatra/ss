📖 Booklet Imposition

The Concept of Imposition

Imposition is the architectural arrangement of printed product pages on the press sheet. This tool automates Saddle-Stitching logic, formatting sequential PDFs so they print correctly when folded down the spine.

Signature Terminology

  • 1-Fold (4 pages/sig): The simplest unit. One physical sheet folded once down the center yields 4 reading pages (Front Cover, Inside Front, Inside Back, Back Cover).
  • 2-Fold (8 pages/sig): Two sheets nested together before folding. Excellent for thin brochures.
  • 4-Fold (16 pages/sig): Four nested sheets. The standard for dense academic booklets to prevent the spine from creeping (bulging).

Geometric Controls

  • Binding Gap: Defines the blank gutter space reserved precisely at the spine for staples or binding glue.
  • Outer Margin: The "bleed" zone required by physical printers to ensure text isn't severed during edge trimming.

⤡ Format & Resize

Academic & Governmental Standards

Digital submission portals enforce stringent geometric and physical data constraints.
  • DPI (Dots Per Inch): A printing metric. 200-300 DPI is standard for legible document scans. Modifying DPI alters the EXIF metadata, not necessarily the display size on a screen.
  • Dimensional Transcoding: Inputting CM or Inches automatically forces the Canvas algorithm to calculate required pixels based on the selected DPI target.
  • KB Constraints: If a target size is set (e.g., Max 50KB), the engine will iteratively execute lossy compression loops until the byte-size falls below the threshold.

✍ Signature Extraction

Alpha Channel Inversion

Extracting ink from physical paper requires programmatic pixel iteration.
  • Heuristic Scan: The algorithm reads the RGBA array of the canvas. It converts RGB data to perceived luminance.
  • Tolerance Threshold: Pixels exhibiting high luminance (the white paper) have their Alpha channel forced to 0 (transparent).
  • Ink Reinforcement: Remaining dark pixels undergo aggressive contrast scaling to generate a stark, digital-ready signature stamp.

◌ Diagnostic WM Removal

Structural vs. Visual Obfuscation

Watermarks exist in two fundamentally distinct architectures within a PDF.
  • Annotation Layer (Easy): Metadata stamps floating above the page. The Structural Scan deletes these arrays entirely.
  • Content Stream (Complex): Text hardcoded into the actual page matrix. This tool deploys spatial heuristics—scanning coordinate geometries to classify objects residing in the Top 15% (Header) or Bottom 15% (Footer)—and applying absolute-white redaction vectors.
  • Limitation: True visual OCR reading is impossible offline. Results rely strictly on mathematical coordinate targeting.

☑ Stamp Verification

Cryptographic Mimicry

Academic and governmental portals frequently distribute documents requiring "Signature Verified" visual status markers.
  • Execution: The engine parses a raw, mathematically defined SVG path (the standard green checkmark) and integrates it directly into the PDF's uncompressed object stream at a designated coordinate grid.
  • Warning: This adds a visual stamp. It does not generate genuine cryptographic RSA signatures.
