📖 Booklet Imposition
Saddle-stitch booklet: Sheets are nested and stapled through the centre fold. Like a magazine — pages 1 and the last page are printed on the same outer sheet, back-to-back.

Signature: A group of sheets folded together. Choosing "4 sheets per signature" nests 4 physical sheets, giving 16 page positions per signature group (4 sheets × 2 sides × 2 columns per side).

Spine / Binding Gap: The blank space in the centrefold reserved for the staple. Increase for thicker paper stock.

Registration Marks: After printing, you need to fold and staple accurately. Marks include a dashed fold guide, a "Signature 1 · Sheet 2 of 4" label so you know the assembly order, and four alignment dots along the spine.

Three Processing Steps:
① Resize — Pages are stretched to fill exactly half the canvas width (277 mm / 2).
② Impose — Pages are reordered onto A4-landscape sheets in the correct saddle-stitch sequence.
③ Marks — Fold guides, "Sig. N · Sheet N of N" labels and binding dots are drawn on every sheet.

◌ Remove Watermark
Where watermarks live in a PDF:
  • Annotation layer — Separate overlay, added by tools like Acrobat stamps. Fully removable.
  • XObject overlays — Embedded graphic templates. Detectable and strippable.
  • Content stream — Drawn directly into the page during original creation. Cannot be removed without re-rasterizing the entire page (needs Ghostscript/server-side tool).
Strategy 1: Strips the Annots array — handles most stamp/sticker watermarks.
Strategy 2: Searches content stream text for your keyword and blanks the region. Works on simple text watermarks.

Always Scan first — the result recommends which strategy applies.

📐 Image Resize
Pixels vs Physical Size: Physical size only has meaning with DPI. Formula: pixels = size × DPI. E.g. 3.5 cm at 200 DPI = 3.5 ÷ 2.54 × 200 ≈ 276 px.

Common exam/govt requirements:
  • Photograph: 3.5 × 4.5 cm, 200 DPI, max 50 KB, JPEG
  • Signature: 3.5 × 1.5 cm, 200 DPI, max 20 KB, JPEG
Target KB: The tool iteratively adjusts JPEG quality via binary search until output is within ~5% of your target.
Keep Aspect Ratio: Changing one dimension auto-scales the other.

⊞ Matrix Calculator
🌐 matrixcalc.org
Matrix: A rectangular grid of numbers with rows (horizontal) and columns (vertical). Written as m×n where m = rows, n = columns.

Square matrix: Same number of rows and columns (2×2, 3×3, etc.). Determinant and inverse only exist for square matrices.

Determinant (det A): A single number that summarises a square matrix. If det = 0, the matrix has no inverse (called "singular").

Inverse (A⁻¹): A matrix such that A × A⁻¹ = Identity matrix. Only exists when det ≠ 0.

Transpose (Aᵀ): Flip rows and columns. Row 1 becomes Column 1.

Rank: The number of linearly independent rows (or columns). Tells you how much "real information" is in the matrix.

RREF (Reduced Row Echelon Form): A standardised form reached by row operations. Used to solve systems of linear equations.

Eigenvalues: Special numbers λ where A×v = λ×v for some non-zero vector v. Supported for 2×2 matrices.

Operations needing two matrices (A and B): Add, Subtract, Multiply. For A+B and A−B, both matrices must have the same dimensions. For A×B, columns of A must equal rows of B.
